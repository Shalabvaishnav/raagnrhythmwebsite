The Foundation

Why Carnatic Music?

Carnatic music forms the foundation of most music we hear today — including film and contemporary styles. A student who understands this system deeply can learn and adapt to any style later, much faster and with more confidence.

Early progress may feel gradual because the focus is on accuracy in pitch, rhythm, and voice control. That strong base pays off for life.

Sings in tune with confidence
Strong sense of rhythm
Clear & stable voice
Better focus & memory
Learn any music faster
Lifelong musical skill

At Home

Practice is the Key

Classes run twice a week — 8 per month. But real progress happens between sessions. Short, consistent practice at home makes all the difference.

Practice Routine

Starting Out
15–30 min daily
After 1–2 months
30+ min daily
Repetitions
4–5 times per lesson
How to Practice
Start slow → increase speed gradually → always use shruti (tanpura / drone)
The Rule: Progress in class + Practice at home = Results

What to Expect

Progress Milestones

With regular, daily practice here is what you can realistically expect:

1 Month
Familiar with all seven swaras and basic exercises
3 Months
Comfortable with scale patterns, voice starts to stabilise
6 Months
Clear improvement in voice clarity, pitch, and confidence

Without consistent practice, progress slows and lessons repeat. Short and daily is better than long and occasional.

For Parents

How to Support Your Child

Your involvement makes a visible difference. You don't need to know music — you just need to be consistent.

🕐Set a fixed practice time each day — treat it like homework
👏Encourage consistency over perfection — effort matters most
🔇Keep the practice space quiet and distraction-free
📅Attend a monthly check-in to see where your child stands
🎤Encourage small recordings every 2–3 months to hear growth
